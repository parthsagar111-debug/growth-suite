How this works

Every number on every page is computed by deterministic code — cohort math, z-test sample sizes, trigger-day rules — never guessed by a model. AI's job is layered on top: multiple named agents per tool interpret, compare, and narrate those numbers, and every tool shows its full dashboard before showing a recommendation.

The one deliberate exception is grading: when an experiment ships and real results come in, the SHIP/KILL/EXTEND call is made by comparing actuals against the thresholds you already committed to — by code, not by a model.

First Response is the one exception to the pattern above — no data upload, no shared memory, just a branching diagnostic chat (max 5 questions, strict-mode branching) that tells you what to check before you touch the other four tools.
